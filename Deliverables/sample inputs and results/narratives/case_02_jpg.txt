Mary Johnson is a 68-year-old woman with type 2 diabetes mellitus who developed lactic acidosis following use of metformin. Her medical history is significant for hypertension, for which she takes aspirin 81 mg daily, lisinopril 10 mg daily, and previously took metformin for diabetes. She has no other medical conditions or allergies. One week ago, Ms. Johnson presented with lactic acidosis, with symptoms including muscle pain, fatigue, and difficulty breathing. Laboratory assessment showed an arterial blood pH of 7.2 and an elevated serum lactate level of 5.0 mmol/L. After metformin was discontinued, the patient's condition improved.
